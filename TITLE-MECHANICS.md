# Title Mechanics — What Actually Won (and What Transfers)

Pulled from the guru's materials — separating what's real from what's just what he happened to do.

## What won (the transferable mechanics)

1. **Formula slots, not clever titles.** Every title he wrote was `Over 50? Do THESE 5 Exercises for ______`. One skeleton, a blank, infinite fills. The skeleton does the work; the fill does the targeting. He never wrote a title from scratch — he filled a slot that had already proven it converts.
2. **The audience is named in the first two words.** "Over 50?" is a filter. It repels everyone else and grabs the person it's for, hard. Nobody has to wonder if the video is for them.
3. **Number + specificity in the promise.** "THESE 5 Exercises." Not "some exercises." Five. Bounded, countable, feels finishable. Same reason "Top 15 Humanoid Robots" beats "Great Humanoid Robots."
4. **The stack he wrote down himself:** fear + age + authority + simplicity, in one line. That's a real formula, and it's why the titles hit.
5. **Escalation ladder.** He had a tame tier and a dark tier — "for Knee Pain" vs. "to Avoid Needing a Walker" vs. "Your Doctor Won't Show You." He knew which tier hit harder and said so out loud ("lean darker, usually slaps harder").
6. **Question-form curiosity loop.** "What happens if you do the HORSE STANCE every day" — an open loop the viewer needs closed. Different mechanism from the listicle, works for a different mood.

## What won for him but doesn't transfer

The fabricated authority ("Top Heart Surgeon Says," "Doctor Tested 211 Types") and the fake outcomes ("melts plaque overnight"). Those aren't hooks, they're claims — false ones, aimed at scared elderly people. That's what makes his channel a countdown timer, and it's why you don't copy his fills.

## What it becomes in our lane

Same six mechanics, no fabrication needed — because in robotics the true thing is already shocking:

| His mechanic | Our version |
|---|---|
| Audience named up front | `Thinking about a home robot?` / `$499/month` |
| Number + bounded promise | `The 3 numbers that decide it` |
| Fear/desire + authority | The authority is the company's own fine print — cite them, not a fake surgeon |
| Escalation ladder | tame: `Is it worth it?` → sharper: `What they don't tell you` → darkest honest: `There's a person inside it` |
| Curiosity loop | `What happens when you cancel the $499 subscription?` |
| One skeleton, infinite fills | `[Product]: What They Don't Tell You` — NEO, Optimus, G1, Figure, forever |

**The core translation:** he manufactured the shocking claim. We find it and cite the primary source. Same click, better retention (because the payoff is real), and no termination risk.

## Standing rule: title vs. body split

The title sells from the sharp tier of the ladder ("What They Don't Tell You", "There's a Person Inside It"). The body pays it off from the optimist-buyer stance ("I'm on team robot — here's the honest math"). Dark door, warm room. The click comes from the ladder; the retention and the subscribe come from not being a complainer once they're inside.
