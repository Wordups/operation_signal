## GLOBAL STYLE (prepend to every shot)

Cinematic documentary b-roll, photorealistic, shallow depth of field, soft natural window light, muted warm palette with dark teal shadows, slow deliberate camera movement, 35mm look, subtle film grain. The robot matches the reference image exactly: slim humanoid, soft beige knit-suit body, calm matte face. Movement is slow, smooth, slightly careful — never fast or menacing. No text, no captions, no watermarks, no brand logos.

## SHOT LIST

**S1 — Hero walk (cold open, 0:00)**
Slow push-in: the robot from the reference image walks unhurried through a sunlit modern living room toward camera, morning light through sheer curtains, dust motes in the air. Hopeful, quiet, "the future arrived softly."

**S2 — The line you're joining (§1, ~1:30)**
Static wide shot: the robot stands perfectly still in a bright minimalist showroom beside a small pedestal, as if waiting to be collected. Long shadow. Feels like a product that exists but hasn't come home yet.

**S3 — Careful hands (§1 specs, ~3:00)**
Close-up: the robot's fabric-covered hands slowly and precisely pick up a single coffee mug from a kitchen counter and place it in a dishwasher rack. Emphasize slowness and care, slight pause before the grip.

**S4 — The operator (§2 Expert Mode, ~4:30)**
Dim modern office at night: a human operator in a VR headset with hand controllers sits before a large monitor showing a softly blurred home interior; their hand movements mirror faintly glowing robot-arm overlays. Blue monitor glow vs warm desk lamp. Intriguing, not sinister.

**S5 — Training fleet (§2 reframe, ~6:30)**
Montage feel, slow lateral dolly: the robot folds a t-shirt on a bed with toddler-like concentration; faint schematic light-lines trace its joints, suggesting every motion is being learned from. Warm bedroom light.

**S6 — Factory scale (§4 wait math, ~12:30)**
Slow aerial-style dolly across a bright clean factory floor: rows of identical humanoid robots in various stages of assembly on carriers, technicians in the distance. Sense of an industry waking up.

**S7 — The boring stack wins (§3 math, ~9:00)**
Cozy real-world kitchen: a round robot vacuum glides across the floor in the foreground while, through a doorway, a human cleaner wipes a counter. Ordinary, warm, dependable — the unglamorous winner.

**S8 — 2028 buy signal (§5 verdict, ~17:30)**
Golden-hour suburban street, slow rising crane shot: a family unloads groceries while the robot from the reference image carries a bag up the walkway beside a kid on a bike. Optimistic near-future, everyday and unremarkable.
