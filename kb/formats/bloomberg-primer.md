# Format teardown — Bloomberg Primer: "Humanoid Robots and the Gap Between Hype and Reality"

*Studied 2026-07-09 (frames sampled at 10% intervals). youtu.be/UQZooauU-FQ — Bloomberg Originals, 24:01, 480K views in 2 months, 5.09M subs. Brian: "replicate this in a way." Emulation target: grammar and structure, never assets.*

## Structure
- 24 min, ~8 named chapters that work as acts: cold open (viral-clip montage) → A CEO and… → The Money → The Robot → How to Train → The World's [competition] → China's Humanoids → Get a Job [factory trials] → Testing H…
- Thesis = literally our lane: the gap between hype and reality. They even cover NEO's teleoperation ("Neo needed some help from real humans").
- Skeleton: **investigation-in-acts** — strong C-3 candidate for video #002/#003 (different from #001's question→teardown→math→verdict).

## Visual grammar (replicable ranked)
1. **Archival inset treatment** — footage never full-bleed; inset ~70% on black canvas, registration crosshairs in the margins, source watermark top-left (e.g. "orikron", "BMW Group"), channel mark top-right, caption boxed below the inset. Attribution as design = credibility as brand. → REPLICATE in prep_broll (our screenshots become "evidence frames").
2. **Boxed kinetic captions** — key lines burned in as tight boxed text, lower-center. → replicate at edit or PIL.
3. **Blueprint isometric diagrams** — flat yellow-line isometrics on black dot grid, mono label chips ("EXPERIENCE LIBRARY"). Matches our design brief ("blueprint style, exploded views"). → slide template for concept sections.
4. **Named chapters as acts** with on-screen chapter cards.
5. Aerial establishing shots + trade-show floor footage — sourced, insettable.
6. NOT replicable solo: field reporting (Berkeley visit), sit-down interviews. Our substitute: the cybersecurity-practitioner lens + primary-document reads.

## Rhythm
- Visual change constantly (est. every 3–8s); voice-over documentary register, calm; captions carry the thesis between shots.
- Mix ratio (sampled): ~70% sourced footage w/ treatment, ~20% graphics/diagrams, ~10% talking heads.
