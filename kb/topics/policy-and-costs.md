# Policy, safety standards, and cost forecasts

*Last verified: 2026-07-09 (video #001 fact-check).*

## GUARD Act (US)
- Introduced June 2026 — Moolenaar / Obernolte / McClellan, House Select Committee on the CCP
- Would BLOCK importation/sale of Chinese-made humanoid + quadruped robots pending national-security review; explicitly targets Unitree
- Sources: chinaselectcommittee.house.gov press release; Washington Times 2026-06-04
- Framing rule: Chinese humanoids face RESTRICTION in the US, not certification — the overseas price war may reach US consumers late or never. "Doesn't change the direction. Changes the date."

## Safety certification
- UL 3300 = consumer/service robot safety standard; OSHA added it to NRTL test-standards list 2025-12-31
- No Chinese home humanoid has UL 3300 or equivalent as of mid-2026
- China published its own first national humanoid-robot standards system Mar 2026 (Xinhua, 2026-03-03)

## Cost-decline forecasts (named sources only — never "analysts say")
- **Goldman Sachs** (Jan 2025): observed humanoid BOM dropped ~40% in ONE year (2023→2024, $50–250K → $30–150K) vs their prior 15–20%/yr expectation
- **BofA Institute** (Apr 2025 + "Physical AI part 2" 2026-03-12): China BOM $35K (2025) → below $17K by 2030; pilot-stage humanoids cost $90–100K to produce, "cut in half by 2030"
- **Morgan Stanley**: China BOM −16% in 2026; long-run average unit cost ~$50K → ~$21K
- DO NOT use "30–50% in 2–3 years consensus" — unattributable, killed in #001 fact-check

## Analogy guardrails
- Robot vacuums: Roomba 980 launched $899 (Sept 2015). 2026 FLAGSHIPS cost MORE ($1,599–1,799 list; ~$999 on sale). Correct framing: "the capability that cost $1,000 in 2015 sells for a couple hundred now" — never "robot vacuums cost $200 now"
- Drones: pre-DJI aerial camera work = five-figure rigs; DJI industrialization → under $1K within a few years
